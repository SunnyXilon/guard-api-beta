# Go-To-Market

## First wedge

Target `UGC platforms and communities` first, especially:

- chat products
- niche communities and forums
- gaming communities
- creator apps with comments or DMs

This wedge is attractive because the pain is urgent, moderation happens in real time, and smaller teams often need a hosted API rather than building trust-and-safety infrastructure themselves.

## Product packaging

Sell the product as a `trust-and-safety API + console`.

Core paid features:

- real-time text moderation API
- tenant policy customization
- review queue and analyst console
- webhook alerts for blocked and reviewable items
- audit logs and downloadable moderation history

Higher-tier enterprise features:

- stricter policy packs for kids/family-safe products
- marketplace anti-fraud presets
- SLA and support guarantees
- private deployment or VPC deployment
- custom taxonomy tuning

## Pricing motion

Start with a usage-based model:

- free trial for developers
- starter tier based on moderated events per month
- growth tier with dashboard, custom thresholds, and webhook events
- enterprise tier with compliance, private deployment, and custom support

Illustrative pricing logic:

- charge per 1,000 moderation events
- add a platform fee for dashboard and case management
- charge premium rates for image/video moderation later

## Why customers will pay

Customers do not only buy a classifier. They buy:

- lower legal and brand risk
- faster moderation turnaround
- fewer moderators needed for simple cases
- configurable thresholds that match their own community rules
- explainability and logs they can show to internal stakeholders

## Sales narrative

Position this product as:

`Real-time trust-and-safety infrastructure for fast-growing community products.`

The strongest differentiation angles for this MVP are:

- low latency for live comments and chat
- tenant-level policy controls
- good handling of scam and abuse workflows
- a clear path to multimodal moderation
